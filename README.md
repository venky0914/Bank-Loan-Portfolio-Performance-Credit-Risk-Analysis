# 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes a bank’s loan portfolio to evaluate **lending performance, borrower behavior, and credit risk patterns**.

Using **SQL for data analysis** and **Tableau for visualization**, an interactive dashboard was developed to provide insights into loan performance, borrower characteristics, and portfolio risk.

The goal of this project is to help stakeholders **monitor lending performance and make data-driven financial decisions**.

---

# 🎯 Business Problem

Banks process thousands of loan applications every year. Without proper analytics, it becomes difficult to:

- Monitor loan portfolio performance
- Identify high-risk borrowers
- Track repayment behavior
- Detect potential loan defaults
- Understand borrower demand patterns

This project provides a **loan analytics dashboard** that enables stakeholders to monitor lending operations and improve credit risk management.

# 🏦 Loan Portfolio Health

| Category | Percentage |
|--------|-----------|
| ✅ Good Loans | **86.2%** |
| ⚠ Bad Loans | **13.8%** |

✔ Good loans include **Fully Paid and Current loans**

Although the portfolio is performing well overall, the **13.8% charged-off loans highlight potential credit risk**.

# 📈 Key Insights

## 📅 Loan Demand Trends
Loan funding shows a **steady increase across months**, indicating growing borrowing demand and seasonal lending patterns.

---

## 👨‍💼 Borrower Employment Analysis
Borrowers with **10+ years of employment** receive the highest number of loan approvals.

**Insight:** Employment stability strongly influences borrower reliability.

---

## 🏠 Home Ownership Analysis
Most loans are issued to customers with:

- Mortgage
- Rental homes

These groups represent the **largest borrower segments**.

---

## 🎯 Loan Purpose Analysis

Top loan purposes include:

- 🏡 Home Improvement
- 🏢 Small Business
- 🚗 Car Loans
- 🛍 Major Purchases

**Insight:**  
Home improvement and small business loans represent the **largest funding categories**.

---

## ⏳ Loan Term Distribution

| Loan Term | Percentage |
|---------|-----------|
| 36 Months | **62.6%** |
| 60 Months | **37.3%** |

Shorter loan terms typically lead to **lower credit risk and faster repayment cycles**.

---

# 📍 Geographic Loan Distribution

Loan activity is concentrated in specific states.

**Business Insight**

Understanding geographic demand helps banks:

- Identify high-growth lending regions
- Optimize regional lending strategies
- Allocate resources more effectively

# 💡 Improvement Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following improvements are recommended:

### 🔹 Strengthen Credit Risk Evaluation
Implement stricter credit scoring models for high-risk borrowers.

### 🔹 Expand High-Demand Loan Segments
Increase focus on **home improvement and small business loans**.

### 🔹 Improve Borrower Risk Monitoring
Use borrower attributes such as **income, employment length, and DTI** to evaluate risk.

### 🔹 Regional Lending Strategy
Expand operations in **high-demand states** while monitoring risk exposure.

### 🔹 Encourage Shorter Loan Terms
Promote **36-month loan products** to reduce long-term credit risk.
